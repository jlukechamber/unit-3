brooklyn trees

New York City, with a population of over 8 million people, is also home to more than 5 million trees. As of 2020, New York City held 44,509 acres of urban tree canopy with 24% of its land covered in trees. In 1995 the city undertook an unprecendented effort to catalog every tree in the city - the first in what is now the decennial New York City tree census. We now have a better understanding than ever of this critical urban forest. You can find out more about the most recent tree census here.
